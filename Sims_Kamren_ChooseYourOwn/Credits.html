At first, I wanted to create a Minecraft story simulator that takes you through various mines, world exploration, and fighting enemies, but I didn’t feel that, that fit me at all. There were a few different story ideas that I came up with, most of which I didn’t find fitting to share with others, but in the end. I chose to go with Who Wants to be a Millionaire, even though I’ve only seen the show so few times. So. this story takes you through being a contestant in the famous show Who Wants to be a Millionaire. It’s not some long story line, not much back story either, you just enter in as a contestant and you try your luck at winning the million-dollar jackpot. I accomplished my goal pretty well in my opinion, by having a multiple-choice quiz (which is what I wanted), a simple story that does not pull too many heart strings and somewhat excites the player, and although I have probably tweaked it over 40 times, I am happy with how it came out. My gradient color choice came out better than the original image that I had set in the background (sadly I realized last minute that we could not use images in our project), the typeface could have been anything so I chose something that was clear to read. I don’t believe the show uses a specific font; I could be wrong on that? The only thing I would change, or wish I had the knowledge to add, would be a counter on the right side of the screen. Just like the show, it would increment as you get questions right. However, I couldn’t find the right tutorials to accomplish this task. Fortunately, at the bottom of the container I did add a money counter, again I just wish that all the prices per question appear as a static separate container on the right side.
